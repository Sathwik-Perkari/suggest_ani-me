Top 10 Isekai Anime With Over Powered MC

BY LAZY UKITO UPDATED ON AUG 25 2023

1. That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime

  • Genres :

    Action, Adventure, Comedy

    Demons, Fantacy, Magic

  • MAL Score : 8.08

"That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime," often abbreviated as "Slime Isekai," is a popular Japanese light novel series written by Fuse and illustrated by Mitz Vah. It falls under the isekai genre, which involves characters being transported from their normal world to a fantasy or parallel world. The series was first serialized as a web novel in 2013 before being adapted into a light novel series and later into various other media formats.

The story follows Satoru Mikami, an ordinary 37-year-old office worker who is unexpectedly killed in a random stabbing incident. However, he finds himself reincarnated in a fantasy world as a slime, a low-level monster known for its typically weak and gelatinous nature. Despite this, Satoru gains unique abilities in his new form that allow him to analyze and mimic other creatures' abilities, as well as a form of shape-shifting.

Embracing his new life as a slime, Satoru adopts the name Rimuru Tempest and begins to explore the world he's been reborn into. He quickly befriends various creatures, including powerful monsters, spirits, and humans, as he aims to create a safe and peaceful community where different species can coexist harmoniously. The story blends action, adventure, and humor as Rimuru gains new allies, faces formidable challenges, and gradually becomes a prominent figure in the world.

"That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime" has been well-received for its engaging world-building, unique take on the isekai genre, and the growth of its protagonist from a seemingly insignificant slime to a formidable force to be reckoned with. It has been adapted into multiple manga adaptations, an anime series, spin-off novels, and video games, contributing to its widespread popularity among fans of fantasy and isekai stories.

2. Over lord

  • Genres :

    Action, Fantacy

    Super natural

  • MAL Score : 8.02

"Overlord" is a Japanese light novel series written by Kugane Maruyama and illustrated by so-bin. It is also a popular anime adaptation and has gained a significant following in the fantasy and isekai genres.

The story is set in the year 2138, where virtual reality massively multiplayer online role-playing games (VRMMORPGs) have become highly advanced. The story centers around the game "Yggdrasil," which is known for its complexity and depth. The game is about to be shut down after running for many years, and the protagonist, Momonga, a powerful and enigmatic undead character, is the last player logged in as the servers are about to go offline.

As the clock strikes midnight and the game servers are supposed to shut down, something unexpected happens. The game world doesn't disappear, and the NPCs (non-player characters) begin to display lifelike behaviors and personalities. The game world of Yggdrasil has somehow become a real and tangible world. Momonga finds himself trapped in this new reality, with his character retaining his powers, personality, and knowledge.

Seizing this opportunity, Momonga takes on the new name of his in-game character, Ainz Ooal Gown, and decides to explore this unfamiliar world. He discovers that the NPCs of his guild's base, the Great Tomb of Nazarick, have come to life with distinct personalities. Ainz, now an immensely powerful undead overlord, sets out to learn more about this new reality, assert his dominance, and uncover the mysteries behind this world's transformation.

As Ainz Ooal Gown explores the world, he seeks to find other players who might have been transported like him and learn about the rules of this new realm. The story blends elements of action, strategy, politics, and intrigue as Ainz navigates the challenges of being an all-powerful overlord while also dealing with the moral and ethical dilemmas that arise from his newfound authority.

"Overlord" delves into themes of identity, power, and morality, often challenging the traditional conventions of fantasy storytelling. The series has received praise for its unique take on the isekai genre, its complex characters, and its exploration of the dynamics between NPCs and the player who is now their ruler. It has a growing fan base and has been adapted into several manga series, an anime adaptation, and other media.

3. The Eminence in Shadow

  • Genres :

    Action, Fantacy, Comedy

  • MAL Score : 8.35

"The Eminence in Shadow" is a Japanese light novel series written by Daisuke Aizawa and illustrated by Touzai. It's also known as "Kage no Jitsuryokusha ni Naritakute!" in Japanese. The story follows Cid Kagenou, a young man who is a huge fan of stories featuring secret organizations, spies, and shadowy figures. He becomes disappointed with his own ordinary life and decides to become a "shadow master," someone who operates behind the scenes to manipulate events and control the world from the shadows, just like the characters in his favorite stories.

Despite lacking any actual abilities, Cid starts orchestrating events and recruiting a group of friends to help him achieve his goal of becoming the ultimate shadow master, using his knowledge of fictional spy tactics and secret agent techniques. The series is known for its comedic and self-aware take on the isekai and fantasy genres, as Cid navigates various challenges and misunderstandings while trying to maintain his facade as the "Eminence in Shadow."

The light novel explores themes of escapism, the contrast between fantasy and reality, and the power of perception. It's a blend of comedy, fantasy, and adventure, and it's popular for its quirky protagonist and the humorous situations that arise from his attempts to become the mastermind he envisions himself to be

4. The Fruit of Evolution: Before I Knew It, My Life Had It Made

  • Genres :

    Adventure, Fantacy, Romance

  • MAL Score : 8.13

"The Fruit of Evolution: Before I Knew It, My Life Had It Made" is a Japanese light novel series written by Hota and illustrated by Huuka Kazabana. The story revolves around Kaname, a young man who finds himself reincarnated in a fantasy world after his death. However, unlike many isekai stories, Kaname's circumstances take an unusual turn.

Upon his reincarnation, Kaname discovers that he has been reborn as a lowly goblin. Goblins in this world are typically seen as weak and expendable creatures, often hunted by adventurers for experience and loot. However, Kaname's situation takes an unexpected twist when he consumes a mysterious and powerful fruit that grants him significant evolutionary abilities.

Using the abilities granted by the fruit, Kaname rapidly evolves and transforms from a simple goblin into a formidable and unique creature. He gains incredible strength, intelligence, and magical abilities, becoming far more powerful than the average denizen of the fantasy world. This evolution also grants him the potential to develop new and rare abilities that set him apart from others.

The series follows Kaname's journey as he navigates the challenges of his new life and explores the world around him. He forms bonds with other creatures, including monsters that would typically be considered his enemies, and gradually changes the perception of goblins in the fantasy world. Kaname's evolution and growth, both physically and emotionally, become central themes as he strives to overcome his humble origins and forge his own path in the fantastical setting.

"The Fruit of Evolution" combines elements of fantasy, adventure, and character development, as Kaname uses his newfound abilities to challenge conventions and reshape his destiny in a world where he was initially considered insignificant.

5. The Rising of the Shield Hero

  • Genres :

    Action, Adventure

    Drama, Fantacy

  • MAL Score : 8.01

"The Rising of the Shield Hero" is a Japanese light novel series written by Aneko Yusagi. The story follows Naofumi Iwatani, a young man who is summoned to a parallel world along with three other individuals. Each of them is chosen to embody one of the four legendary heroes, equipped with a weapon representing their role: sword, spear, bow, and shield.

Naofumi finds himself designated as the Shield Hero, a role he initially considers underpowered compared to the other heroes. His early optimism is shattered when he is falsely accused of a crime and betrayed by a fellow companion, leading to his isolation and distrust of others. Without traditional offensive abilities, Naofumi must rely on his shield's defensive capabilities to survive and grow stronger.

As the story progresses, Naofumi learns to adapt and maximize the unique abilities of the shield, discovering that it has hidden potential that goes beyond defense. He forms unlikely alliances with demi-human characters who have faced discrimination, and together, they rise against powerful adversaries and unravel the mysteries of the world they've been summoned to.

"The Rising of the Shield Hero" explores themes of perseverance, resilience, and the consequences of prejudice. Naofumi's character development is a central focus, as he transforms from a distrusting and cynical individual into a more open-hearted and determined hero. The series delves into the complexities of moral decisions, personal growth, and the impact of trauma on one's perspective.

The light novel has been adapted into manga, anime, and other media, gaining popularity for its unique approach to the isekai genre by featuring a protagonist who relies on unconventional strengths and struggles against adversity while challenging the typical hero narrative.

6. Arifureta: From Commonplace to World's Strongest

  • Genres :

    Action, Adventure

    Fantacy, Harem

  • MAL Score : 8.8

"Arifureta: From Commonplace to World's Strongest" is a Japanese light novel series written by Ryo Shirakome. The story revolves around Hajime Nagumo, an ordinary high school student who, along with his classmates, is transported to a fantasy world with the goal of becoming heroes and saving the world.

However, unlike his classmates who possess impressive magical abilities and talents, Hajime is initially considered weak and only possesses transmutation magic, which allows him to manipulate the properties of objects. Due to his perceived lack of combat potential, he is often ostracized and mistreated by his peers. Despite this, he remains determined to survive and find a way back home.

During a perilous dungeon expedition, Hajime, betrayed by one of his classmates and falls into the depths of the dungeon, suffering near-fatal injuries. Through sheer determination and resourcefulness, he manages to survive and overcome numerous challenges. Using his transmutation magic, he crafts powerful weapons and equipment from the dungeon's materials, gradually evolving into a formidable warrior.

As Hajime gains strength, he becomes more focused on his personal goals rather than the heroic aspirations of his classmates. He embarks on a journey of revenge and self-discovery, ultimately seeking to return to his original world. Along the way, he encounters various allies and adversaries, each with their own motivations and secrets.

"Arifureta" is known for its darker and more mature themes compared to other isekai stories. It explores themes of survival, redemption, and personal growth, as Hajime transforms from an underestimated and mistreated individual into a powerful and self-reliant force. The series also delves into the complexities of morality and the consequences of one's actions.

The light novel has been adapted into manga, anime, and other media, and it has garnered attention for its action-packed sequences, unique protagonist development, and its departure from the typical isekai hero trope

7. Knight's & Magic

  • Genres :

    Action, Adventure, Fantacy

  • MAL Score : 7.16

"Knight's & Magic" is a Japanese light novel series written by Hisago Amazake-no and illustrated by Kurogin. The story follows Ernesti Echevarria, a young boy who dies in a car accident in his own world and is then reborn in a fantasy world called Erald. In this new world, magic and medieval-style warfare using giant robots known as Silhouette Knights are prevalent.

Ernesti, now known as Eru, retains his memories and passion for mecha and robotics from his previous life. He quickly becomes enamored with the idea of creating and piloting his own Silhouette Knight, which he sees as the perfect fusion of his two interests: magic and mecha. With his knowledge of advanced technology from his previous world, Eru seeks to revolutionize the Silhouette Knight design and enhance their performance.

Driven by his enthusiasm and talent, Eru becomes a prodigious magic user and a skilled engineer, incorporating modern engineering concepts into the creation of his Silhouette Knight. He gathers a group of friends and allies who share his vision and together, they embark on a journey to reshape the world of Erald by introducing new technological advancements.

"Knight's & Magic" blends elements of fantasy, mecha, and isekai genres. The series is known for its detailed descriptions of the magic-based technology, intricate world-building, and exciting battles involving the giant robots. Eru's passion and determination to combine his love for mecha with the magical world he's in make for an engaging and unique storyline.

The light novel has been adapted into manga, and its anime adaptation has further brought its mecha battles and fantastical world to life. "Knight's & Magic" appeals to fans of both fantasy and science fiction, offering an imaginative take on a world where magic and technology intersect.

8. Cautious Hero: The Hero Is Overpowered but Overly Cautious

  • Genres :

    Action, Adventure

    Comedy, Fantacy

  • MAL Score : 7.57

"Cautious Hero: The Hero Is Overpowered but Overly Cautious" is a Japanese light novel series written by Light Tuchihi and illustrated by Saori Toyota. The story centers around Seiya Ryuuguuin, a hero who is summoned to a fantasy world to save it from a looming threat. However, Seiya is not your typical heroâ€”he is excessively cautious to an extreme degree.

Seiya's cautious nature leads him to train obsessively and prepare exhaustively for every possible scenario, no matter how unlikely. He refuses to engage in any battle or situation unless he is absolutely certain of victory, even if it means going to extreme lengths to ensure his safety. This often results in comedic and over-the-top moments, as Seiya takes even the smallest risks seriously and approaches every challenge with meticulous planning.

Despite his extreme caution, Seiya's thorough preparations and incredible power make him an overwhelmingly strong hero. He quickly becomes adept at defeating powerful enemies with ease, often using his over-the-top caution to outwit his opponents. Throughout the story, Seiya's interactions with other characters, who are often baffled or frustrated by his overly cautious behavior, lead to humorous situations and unexpected outcomes.

The series balances comedy with action and adventure, as Seiya's quirky personality clashes with the conventional hero archetype. "Cautious Hero" plays with the expectations of the isekai and hero genres, turning the concept of an overpowered hero on its head by emphasizing the hero's excessive caution and preparedness. The series offers a fresh take on the isekai formula, providing laughs and entertainment through Seiya's unique approach to being a hero.

9. How Not to Summon a Demon Lord

  • Genres :

    Comedy, Fantacy

    Magic, Harem

  • MAL Score : 7.03

"How Not to Summon a Demon Lord" is a Japanese light novel series written by Yukiya Murasaki and illustrated by Takahiro Tsurusaki. The story revolves around Takuma Sakamoto, a socially awkward gamer who gets transported into the world of his favorite video game, "Cross Reverie," in the body of his in-game character, Diablo.

In the fantasy world, Takuma finds himself possessing immense magical power and the appearance of a powerful demon lord. However, due to his real-life social anxiety, he struggles to communicate and interact with others. This becomes particularly challenging when two girls, a summoner named Rem and a pantherian named Shera, accidentally use a spell that was supposed to enslave him, but instead, they find themselves magically bound to him.

As the overpowered demon lord Diablo, Takuma must navigate his new reality, pretending to be the character he played in the game while dealing with the personalities and intentions of Rem and Shera. Despite his inexperience with social situations, he tries to act confident and assertive, leading to comedic misunderstandings and awkward interactions.

The series is known for its comedic elements, harem dynamics, and fantasy adventure. Diablo's attempts to maintain his image as a fearsome demon lord, despite his inner insecurities, lead to amusing situations and unexpected character growth. As the story progresses, Takuma's relationships with Rem, Shera, and other characters deepen, and he gradually becomes more comfortable with his role in the fantasy world.

"How Not to Summon a Demon Lord" plays with the isekai trope by having the protagonist embody a powerful character while struggling with real-world personal issues. It combines fantasy action, humor, and character development, making it an engaging and entertaining series for fans of both the isekai and comedy genres.

10. In Another World With My Smartphone

  • Genres :

    Fantacy, Adventure

    Harem, Romance

  • MAL Score : 6.45

"In Another World With My Smartphone" is a Japanese light novel series written by Patora Fuyuhara and illustrated by Eiji Usatsuka. The story revolves around Touya Mochizuki, a teenage boy who dies accidentally and is reincarnated into a fantasy world. However, due to a divine mistake, he's allowed to take his smartphone with him to the new world.

In this fantasy realm, Touya discovers that he possesses a unique advantage - his smartphone, which works perfectly in the new world. With access to advanced technology, information, and communication, Touya quickly becomes a prodigious magic user and an influential figure in the world. He uses his smartphone to learn languages, access maps, and even create new magical spells.

Touya's journey involves exploring the new world, meeting various characters, and becoming involved in political and magical conflicts. His smartphone helps him form alliances, communicate across vast distances, and gather information efficiently. With his immense magical power and the knowledge from his previous life, he solves problems and helps others in need.

The series blends fantasy, adventure, and some slice-of-life elements as Touya adapts to his new life and uses his smartphone to its fullest potential. It's known for its light-hearted tone, harem dynamics (as Touya attracts the attention of multiple girls), and the juxtaposition of modern technology within a medieval fantasy setting.

"In Another World With My Smartphone" offers a mix of fantasy and wish-fulfillment, as the protagonist leverages his smartphone's capabilities to navigate the challenges of the new world and contribute positively to the lives of those around him. The series often explores themes of friendship, personal growth, and the consequences of wielding advanced knowledge in a different context.
